Patient Information and Consent Form

Please read the following information, complete the required details and submit the form before your appointment. If you have any questions or concerns before submitting the form, or would prefer to use a paper form, please feel free to get in touch.

  • You will be seen by a qualified Osteopath who is registered with the General Osteopathic Council
  • You are welcome to have someone else of your choice present during appointment. Patients under the age of 18 will always need to have a chaperone present.
  • The osteopath will ask you detailed questions about the presenting complaint and also about other aspects of general health, past and present.
  • A physical examination will take place involving: you performing some simple movements; the osteopath observing and using their hands to assess the condition of the muscles and joints; and if necessary other specific tests such as taking blood pressure or reflexes. The osteopath will require to see the area they are treating which will often require the removal of some items of outer clothing. If you are uncomfortable removing any particular item of clothing please inform the osteopath and they will do their utmost to adapt.
  • The osteopath will make a diagnosis and discuss this with you. If there is any reason the osteopath does not think osteopathic treatment will be of benefit they will explain why and give advice as how best to proceed. In these cases it may be helpful to contact your GP and we ask your consent for this.
  • Treatment involves manual therapy (i.e. the osteopath using their hands) and there are a variety of techniques used within a session e.g massage, mobilisation and manipulation. The osteopath will explain the process of the technique, the reason for performing the technique, and ensure you are happy to proceed. Please do not hesitate to ask the osteopath to stop the treatment and explain the reason for anything they say or do during the session.
  • It normally takes more than one session for the symptoms to resolve, the number of treatments vary from person to person and it depends on the nature of the condition.
  • Exercises and other advice will normally be given to you in order to speed up the recovery and maintain health.
  • After a treatment, it is not unusual to feel sore or uncomfortable for approximately 24 hours. Please feel free to contact your osteopath if you are concerned about any post treatment soreness.
